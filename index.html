The smallest team in software history is now the right team.

02 — The weave

Three or four humans. A weave of agents around them.

Every Endoloom pod is small by design. A founding team of three to four people holds the seats where judgment, taste, and trust live. Agents run continuously around them, scanning categories, drafting specs, writing code, running outbound. The result is the leverage of a fifty-person company at the size of a band.

The output is evidence-backed target selection, not a hunch.

06 — Operating ethics

Hardcore on conviction. Strict on integrity.

Many of the people we want to talk to have signed serious agreements — NDAs, IP assignments, non-competes. We treat those agreements as load-bearing. The whole thesis is that domain knowledge wins, but domain knowledge is not the same as proprietary information, and we hold the line.

✓ WHAT WE ASK FOR

Public-domain market judgment. What you have observed about a category — pricing patterns, common failure modes, the shape of buyer pain — that you would tell any peer at a conference. The kind of insight a senior operator carries with them as professional knowledge.

✓ HOW WE WORK

Applications are confidential. We never display individual names publicly. Any background or affiliation you share goes only to the cohort selection team. References are checked on your terms, not before.

✗ WHAT WE NEVER ASK FOR

Confidential documents. Specific customer data. Pricing or roadmap detail covered by an NDA. Code or designs that belong to a current or former employer. If in doubt, don't share it. The wedge has to be built clean or it can't be built at all.

✗ NON-COMPETES & GARDEN LEAVE

If a non-compete or garden-leave clause covers the category a pod is attacking, we'll wait it out with you. The cohort runs in waves; the window doesn't close in a month. Talk to us early, build legally, and start when you are clear.

07 — Straight answers

What a sharp operator asks first.

What does it actually mean to "found a pod"? +
You are a founder. Each Endoloom pod is a real company, incorporated separately, with you holding founding equity and running it. Endoloom provides the validated target, the agent stack, the shared infrastructure (legal, finance, hiring, cloud and tooling credits), the senior advisor bench, and seed-stage capital. In exchange, the cohort holds a studio stake. You are the CEO of your pod, not an employee of Endoloom.
How does Endoloom make money? +
Cohort-level equity across every pod. We deliberately don't make money from fees, services, or pod-level expenses — we win when the pods win. The shared infrastructure exists to lower the marginal cost of each new pod and to make the founders' lives easier, not to extract margin.
Why all open seats? Don't you have anyone yet? +
Cohort 01 is being assembled right now. That's the point. We'd rather show you an honest, open pod board than fill seats with names to look credible. You can join the cohort at the same time as the founder sitting across from you. That's a feature of the AI-native moment — small founding teams, formed now, are how the next great software companies get built.
I'm under a non-compete or NDA. Should I still apply? +
Yes — and read the operating ethics section above. We treat your existing agreements as load-bearing. If a non-compete covers the pod's target category, we'll wait it out with you. If you have to be careful about what you share, we won't ask you to share it. Domain judgment is the qualification; proprietary information is never the input.
I have the engineering chops but not a specific domain. Can I still be a Founding Engineer? +
Yes. The Founding Engineer seat is paired with a Founding Lead who carries the domain. You bring the production-grade taste and the agent-stack fluency. You'll also have the option to move between pods in the early weeks if the chemistry isn't right — better to optimize for fit than to force the first match.
Why "founding" and not just "early-stage"? +
Because founding equity, founding accountability, and founding upside are all materially different from joining a company at employee #15. The whole AI-native moment is that a team this small, today, can build what used to take fifty people — and the people in that small team should share in the outcome accordingly. The word matters because the deal matters.
Where do your numbers come from? +
Incumbent published pricing (2025–2026), Gartner and Mordor Intelligence market data, G2 and Gartner Peer Insights review corpora, KPMG supply-chain research, and reputable press (Computer Weekly, TechCrunch, TechTarget) for funding and failed-implementation figures. Where a figure is a range or an estimate, we label it as such. Precision over polish.
